# 🔢 Lesson 4: Numbers and Math

Robotics is 90% math. Whether you are calculating the distance to a planet or the angle of a VEX motor, you need Python to do the heavy lifting.

## 1. The 5-Year-Old Spark ⚡
Python is the world's most powerful calculator. You give it the numbers and the symbols (like `+` or `-`), and it gives you the answer instantly.

## 2. The Deep Dive 🤿
Python handles two main types of numbers:
1. **Integers (`int`):** Whole numbers like `5`, `-10`, or `1000`.
2. **Floats (`float`):** Numbers with decimals like `3.14`, `0.5`, or `-9.8`.

**The Basic Operators:**
* `+` (Addition)
* `-` (Subtraction)
* `*` (Multiplication)
* `/` (Division)
* `**` (Exponent/Power - e.g., `2**3` is 8)

## 3. Side-by-Side Mirror 🪞

**Python:**
```python
distance = 10 / 2  # Result is 5.0 (Python turns it into a float automatically)
```

**Older Languages:**
In some older languages, `10 / 3` might just give you `3` because they throw away the remainder! Python is "smarter" and gives you `3.333...` unless you tell it otherwise.
